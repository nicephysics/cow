The Ladder

The cave extends into a larger cave. It has glittering ‘stars’ on the top of the ceiling, which is around 10 meters high! There is a small pond near you, and you bend your head to drink it. Ah, how refreshing! You continue to open your mouth, now in awe, and gaze at the beautiful sight for a few seconds.
Then something catches your attention. A ladder? Is that a ladder? You rarely see ladders in the farm, as everything is so flat there. Nevertheless, you are certain that it is a ladder. The ladder stretched to the exact top of the ceiling, and it is balancing against the wall. At the middle of the ladder is a hole in the wall, and something glittering inside. That catches your attention and suddenly, you really want to get it.
You walk towards the ladder and see a piece of wood on the ground next to the ladder. Carved on it, there were a few words:

This uniform ladder,
12 metres long and 100 kg,
is leaning on the vertical wall,
60° from the ground.

The ladder can withstand only a maximum friction force of 1000 N before slipping on the ground.

Ah, you think. The ladder might slip on the ground if you climb on it to a certain height, but you just don’t know what height. These values should do it, to calculate the maximum height you can climb, if you assume that the wall is smooth and perfectly vertical. You know that you weigh 40 kg.
Being the first in your farm class, you quickly calculate the distance and realise you cannot climb to the hole. Sadly, you walk away from the ladder.
What is the maximum height you can climb to just before the ladder topples down? (Round your answer to 3 s.f.)

Hint: Draw a diagram. Remember to include units in the answer!
