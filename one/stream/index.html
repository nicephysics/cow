The Stream

“Crack!” You jump, as you hear a noise amongst the pin-drop silence. You look to the left and notice a small crevice in the biggest of the many small lakes in your vision. Wow, you never noticed this before! It seems to be a small cave stream. You ignore the noise (a thud echoes in the distance) as you strain your eyes, finding a way to enter the stream. The hole seems just wide enough for a small-sized cow like you...
Suddenly, you spot a huge juicy cob of corn around 15 metres away. Why is it there, you wonder. Walking to it, you try to pick it up using two of your feet and promptly drop it in the water (you aren’t so nimble after all). It bloops up and down on the clear water, its many kernels glittering in the dark. Maybe if the corn floats on water... you could use it as a raft?
The corn felt around 400 g when you picked it up earlier, and you know it occupies a volume similar to all of your stomachs (40 L). You wonder if it was really really real. Can the corn support you if you stand on it?

Provide the density of you and the corn combined, assuming no part of you touches the water, in kg/m³. (Round your answer to 3 s.f.)
